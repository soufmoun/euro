## About Paris
Paris is more affordable than you think! While known as an expensive city, our tested budget strategies let you experience the magic of Paris without financial stress. From free museum days to affordable neighborhood eats, this guide shows you how to do Paris right on a budget.

## Daily Budget Breakdown
- **Accommodation**: €30-45 (hostel or budget hotel in Montmartre)
- **Food**: €22-30 (bakeries, markets, and bistros)
- **Transport**: €5-8 (metro daily pass)
- **Activities**: €8-12 (museums, attractions)
- **Total**: €65-85 per day

## Free & Cheap Attractions
1. **Eiffel Tower views** from Champ de Mars (free)
2. **Notre-Dame exterior** and surrounding area (free)
3. **Sacré-Cœur Basilica** and Montmartre streets (free)
4. **Louvre Museum** (free first Sunday of month, €17 otherwise)
5. **Seine River walks** and bridges (free)
6. **Père Lachaise Cemetery** (free)
7. **Free walking tours** (tip-based)

## Affordable Accommodation Areas
- **Montmartre**: Artistic vibe, good hostels (€30-40/night)
- **Latin Quarter**: Student area, affordable eats (€35-50/night)
- **11th Arrondissement**: Local feel, good value (€32-47/night)
- **Avoid**: Champs-Élysées area (overpriced)

## Budget Food Guide
**Breakfast**: Boulangerie croissant + coffee (€4-6)
**Lunch**: Market sandwich or falafel (€6-9)
**Dinner**: Bistro plat du jour (€12-18)
**Groceries**: Carrefour or Monoprix for snacks

## Money-Saving Tips
- Buy metro carnets (10 tickets) instead of single tickets
- Visit museums on first Sundays for free entry
- Eat away from tourist areas for better prices
- Use Paris Museum Pass if visiting 3+ museums
- Walk between nearby attractions to save transport

## Sample 3-Day Budget
- Accommodation: €105 (€35/night hostel)
- Food: €60 (€20/day)
- Transport: €15 (metro passes)
- Activities: €30 (2 paid museums)
- **Total**: €210 for 3 days
