## About Rome
Rome offers incredible value with free ancient sites and affordable, delicious food. This guide shows you how to experience Rome's 2,000 years of history without the high costs that scare many travelers away.

## Daily Budget Breakdown
- **Accommodation**: €25-40 (hostel in Trastevere)
- **Food**: €18-25 (pizza, pasta, markets)
- **Transport**: €4-6 (daily metro/bus)
- **Activities**: €8-10 (museums, sites)
- **Total**: €55-75 per day

## Free Ancient Sites
1. **Pantheon** (completely free)
2. **Roman Forum** exterior views (free)
3. **St. Peter's Basilica** (free)
4. **Spanish Steps** and Trevi Fountain (free)
5. **Appian Way** walking (free)
6. **Piazzas** like Navona and Campo de' Fiori (free)

## Affordable Neighborhoods
- **Trastevere**: Authentic, great food (€28-42/night)
- **Monti**: Central but affordable (€32-48/night)
- **Prati**: Near Vatican, good value (€30-45/night)
- **Avoid**: Areas immediately around Termini station

## Eating on a Budget
**Breakfast**: Cornetto + cappuccino (€3-5)
**Lunch**: Pizza al taglio (by slice) (€4-7)
**Dinner**: Trattoria pasta dish (€10-15)
**Wine**: House wine (€4-6/glass)

## Cost-Saving Strategies
- Book Colosseum tickets online to avoid queues
- Churches have free art (replace some museum visits)
- Drink from public water fountains (nasoni)
- Buy Roma Pass for transport + museum discounts
- Visit Vatican Museums on last Sunday (free)

## 4-Day Budget Example
- Accommodation: €140 (€35/night)
- Food: €80 (€20/day)
- Transport: €18 (Roma Pass)
- Activities: €40 (Colosseum, Vatican)
- **Total**: €278 for 4 days
